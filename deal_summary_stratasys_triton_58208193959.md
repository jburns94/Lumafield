## The Opportunity: Why Triton

### Business Problem
Stratasys Direct's **QC lab in Belton is severely bottlenecked**:
- 6 inspectors with 3-4 FARO arms (just purchased a 4th)
- Missing delivery dates and promised dates
- Customer confidence suffering (supplier scorecard drops, lost bidding eligibility)
- Management is tasking them to find automated inspection solutions
- Lead times swing wildly (can go from smooth to 10-day backlogs in a single week)

### Volume Justification
From Zac Chin's analysis of 2025 inspection data:
- **24,000 total inspections** performed in 2025
- **20,000** of those have max dimension <11 inches (fit in Neptune/Triton)
- **99%+ are ITAR** parts
- **Top 4 part numbers alone** = 3,876 inspections/year = **242 working days** of scanning (full 8-hour shift)
- Scan times: FDM ~20 min, SAF/powder ~90 min
- Average scan time ~30 min
- Charging ~$20-$100 per inspection depending on complexity

### Why Triton Over Neptune
1. **Automation**: 24-pallet infeed conveyor enables lights-out operation
2. **Throughput**: Effectively 3x by adding unmanned 2nd and 3rd shifts
3. **Night shift**: Belton has a light night shift that could reload the Triton
4. **ITAR included**: Triton comes with on-prem server (ITAR-compliant) as standard
5. **Recipe-driven**: Can develop scan recipes on Neptune, deploy directly to Triton
6. **Cost consolidation**: ITAR solution cost is baked into Triton pricing vs. separate add-on for Neptune

### Expansion Vision (Andrew Carter's Plan)
1. **Phase 1**: Get ITAR solution on current Neptune → prove QC inspection workflow → get Stephanie and inspectors onboard
2. **Phase 2**: Replace Neptune with **Triton** at Belton for automated inspection
3. **Phase 3**: Move **Neptune to Minnesota** HQ → give broader Stratasys access (R&D, software, print quality teams = potentially 30+ users)
4. **Long-term**: Stratasys corporate could want their own scanner
