## 5. Pain Points & Identified Needs

### Critical Inspection Challenges
1. **Concentricity Measurement:** FluidTech cannot measure the concentricity between thread levels and lower bore levels in their manifold cavities. This is a critical dimension - if threads are off-axis relative to bore levels, the installed valve will bind and fail to function properly.

2. **Perpendicularity Measurement:** Cannot measure perpendicularity of pitch diameter relative to bore levels. Requirement is within 0.001 inches (1 thou). Concentricity requirement is within 0.0009-0.001 inches.

3. **Bore Diameter Tolerances:** Plus or minus 1 thou (0.001") on cavity diameters. Not overly tight, but critical for O-ring sealing between hydraulic fluid zones.

4. **Internal Connection Verification:** Currently using wooden dowel sticks cut to depth to verify drill connections are made properly. This method cannot verify offset drillings that come in at angles. These connections are critical for hydraulic flow paths.

5. **Porosity Detection:** Porosity in aluminum castings/billets goes undetected until unit fails in the field. Porosity near the surface is sometimes caught visually during machining. Sub-surface porosity causes failures roughly once every 3-5 years, but when it happens it typically affects an entire production batch ("campaign"), potentially requiring complete re-manufacturing.

6. **Manual Inspection Bottleneck:** Current manual inspection takes 10-20 minutes per part (spot check) and up to 25 minutes for full inspection. Operators doing inspection are taken away from value-added machine loading work. Current sampling rate is 1 in 10 parts for in-process inspection, 100% on first article only.

### What They Want
- Automated, inline volumetric inspection to replace/augment manual inspection
- Ability to inspect 100% of parts (or close to it) rather than 1-in-10 sampling
- Free up operator labor for machine loading and other value-added work
- CAD overlay / mesh comparison for validating new product designs
- Thread inspection capabilities (pitch diameter, concentricity with bores)
- Porosity detection for aluminum manifolds
- Statistical Process Control (SPC) integration
- Easy-to-use system that doesn't require CT expert operators
- Cloud-based data sharing between Kansas and Syracuse facilities
- Conveyor/inline system for minimal operator intervention
- System that can handle high-mix production (auto-recognize different parts/programs)

## 8. Product Fit & Technical Considerations

- **Part Fit:** FluidTech's average part (4.5" x 5" x 7.25") fits well. Largest parts (8" x 10" x 24") can be accommodated by orienting properly.
- **Penetration Power:**
  - Standard (225kV): ~100mm / ~4 inches of aluminum
