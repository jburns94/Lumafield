### Known Technical Challenges

- All components are **similar grades of steel** — balls, screw, nut, and insert will appear very similar on X-ray
- **Magnets** present on the assembly (samarium cobalt + ceramic/rare earth) — below the area of interest, cropped out of current scanning
- **S-track inserts** create areas where balls are harder to detect (they pass from end of helix back to start of track)
- Current system requires manual **histogram adjustment** per scan — operator skill-dependent
- Current cylindrical cut plane approach requires **manual drawing** per scan — different operators produce inconsistent results

## Current State & Pain Points

### Hardware Reliability
- NSI CT scanner has had the **pump fail 3 times in ~2 years**
- Running on **old operating system and old hardware** — upgrade quoted at ~$60,000
- Machine is **physically oversized** (~3x the footprint of a Neptune) for the parts being scanned
- MTI bought a **backup pump** as a stopgap — not currently in a "line down" situation

### Process Inefficiency
- **Two-facility workflow**: Ball screws manufactured in Muskego → shipped to Pleasant Prairie for CT → shipped back to Muskego for final packing/shipping
- **Losing ~1-2 days per batch** just in transit between facilities (1 hour apart, dedicated courier)
- **5-10 minutes of manual markup per ball screw** on the workstation (drawing cylindrical cut plane, adjusting histogram)
- **1 dedicated employee** spending significant portion of their week just reviewing/counting scans
- Python-based ball counting code has become **buggy** — they've reverted to **manual visual counting** of 2D images
- Andy Hohs has been **rewriting Python into LabVIEW** — additional engineering effort being diverted

### Business Risk
- Defense/aerospace customer with **strict 100% inspection mandate**
- **Business continuity risk**: If scanner goes down, MTI's customer line is impacted — "in some ways immeasurable" cost
- **Staffing shortages** — lean staff, labor reallocation is critical
- No backup scanning capability if NSI goes down beyond pump failure

4. **Eliminate Manual Steps**: No manual cut plane drawing, no histogram adjustment, no manual counting
5. **Single-Location Workflow**: Scanner at Muskego eliminates shipping between facilities
