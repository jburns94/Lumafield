### Call 6: "Lumafield x Disney Imagineering — Intro Scan Discussion" (2026-03-05)

- **Duration**: 59 minutes
- **Gong URL**: https://us-20309.app.gong.io/call?id=1974518773983495426

- **Disney Attendees**: Joel Peavy (Executive R&D Imagineer), Steven Pickles (R&D Imagineer Principal), Jack Wetherell (Lead R&D Imagineer)

**Summary**: This is the most substantive and recent call on the deal. Jake and Jacob (the new account team) held a detailed technical discussion with Disney's R&D leadership. The NDA was already signed. Disney shared details about their robotic character program (BDX droids) and the specific battery scanning needs.

**Key Discussion Points**:

**Disney's R&D Environment**:
- Disney Imagineering R&D is strictly a research and development group. Very little of what they build ends up in production — that's by design.
- Their process is not well-defined: they use highly skilled people in specific areas to rapidly iterate, build prototypes, prove concepts work, then hand off to vendors for production engineering.
- Joel described their world as "fraught with problems — some unanticipated, some anticipated" because they're on the "bleeding edge."
- They have no existing CT scanning experience. Traditional approach to diagnosing issues is full physical disassembly.
- Both Joel and Jack are physicists by training, so they understand CT/X-ray technology well but have never used the machines.

**The Battery Use Case**:
- Steven developed a new lithium chemistry battery with an outside vendor for mobile robots (BDX droids — the robot characters seen at Disney parks).
- The battery is a pack (multiple cells wired together), not individual cells. They want to see:
  - How cells are wired together
  - Where the PC board is and how it's connected
  - Internal structure of the pack
  - How things are soldered/welded
  - Anode-cathode overhang on individual cells
- They have very few of these pre-production batteries and cannot afford to destroy one for inspection.
- The battery is roughly the form factor of a standard pack — Jack showed it on camera.
- After a year of robot service, they'd also want to re-scan to see how the battery has fared.

**Broader Use Cases Identified**:
- Custom PCBs, custom boards, wiring, connectors inside robots
- Cameras, motors, motor components
- Assembly verification (cables mounted correctly, etc.)
- Vendor/supplier quality validation
- Safety validation for parts going into theme parks
- Failure analysis on any component

**Software Demo**:
- Jacob demonstrated Voyager software showing:
  - Brushless DC motor scan with multi-material visualization (copper, neodymium magnets, steel)
  - DJI Mini drone scan showing board-to-board connectors, assembly verification
  - PCB solder/BGA quality inspection at high resolution
  - 18650 battery scan showing AC overhang, tab welds, jelly roll
  - Measurement/dimensioning tools with edge detection
  - Cloud-native collaboration features
  - Auto-mode scanning (set time, machine determines all parameters)

**Safety & Value Drivers**:
- Joel emphasized: "Safety is paramount at Disney." They deliver products to parks (their internal customers) and must ensure quality.
- As they adopt new vendors and technology (especially in robotics, which is accelerating), CT provides a tool to vet vendor quality.
- Value spans: failure analysis, upfront safety, ongoing product verification and validation.
- Steven: "Proving that the quality is up to a certain standard would go a long way to laying concerns over continued use of batteries."

**On-Prem Deployment**:
- Joel raised concern about the word "cloud" scaring Disney's security people.
- Jacob confirmed Lumafield has an on-prem option, used by ITAR-compliant companies and big tech companies.
- They can work with IT to enable collaborative experience within the organization via on-prem server.

**Physical Specifications Discussed**:
- Neptune scanner: about the size of a refrigerator on its side, ~3,000 lbs, on wheels (needs rigging team to move), single 120V outlet.
- Scan time: depends on material density. 5-min scan for low-density items, few hours for motors with copper/neodymium. Trade-off is noise level.
- Resolution: function of part position relative to source/detector. Voxel size driven by geometry, noise driven by scan time.

- Jake visiting Disney in Glendale the following Wednesday to:
  - See the BDX robot workspace
  - Pick up the battery sample for scanning
- Battery to be scanned at Lumafield's LA (El Segundo) or SF office.
- Follow-up call to discuss part dimensions, sizing, materials before shipping.
- Steven expressed excitement: "There's definitely information out of seeing the inside of things that I would not otherwise get."
- Joel: "Didn't realize the suite of software tools you had — a lot of different ways of looking at things."
- Jack: "Most exciting thing would be to see our battery scanned and get hands-on experience with the software."
